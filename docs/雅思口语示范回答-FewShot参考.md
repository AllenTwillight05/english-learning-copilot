# 雅思口语示范回答（Few-Shot 参考）

以下示例用于投喂到 system prompt 的"示例对话"中，让 LLM 学会 "好的回答长什么样"。

## Part 1 — 个人问题（简短回答，2-3句为宜）

### P1-01: Hometown

```
Examiner: Let's talk about your hometown. Where is your hometown?
Test-taker: I'm from Hangzhou， a city in eastern China. It's quite well-known for West Lake and its beautiful scenery.

Examiner: What do you like most about your hometown?
Test-taker: I think the best thing is the natural environment. We have mountains， lakes， and tea plantations all around， so it feels very peaceful even though it's a big city.

Examiner: Has your hometown changed much since you were a child?
Test-taker: Definitely. When I was young， it was much quieter. Now there are more high-rise buildings， a new subway system， and a lot more tourists. It's more modern but also more crowded.

Examiner: Do you think you'll continue to live there in the future?
Test-taker: I'm not sure honestly. I love my hometown， but I might move to a bigger city like Shanghai for better job opportunities. I'll probably visit often though.
```

### P1-02: Work or Study

```
Examiner: Do you work or are you a student?
Test-taker: I'm currently a third-year student at university. I'm majoring in computer science.

Examiner: Why did you choose that subject?
Test-taker: I've always been interested in how technology works. Also， the job prospects in this field are quite good， so it seemed like a practical choice.

Examiner: What do you like most about your studies?
Test-taker: I really enjoy the problem-solving aspect. When I'm coding and I finally figure out a bug， it feels really satisfying.
```

### P1-03: Hobbies

```
Examiner: What do you do in your free time?
Test-taker: I spend a lot of my free time reading， especially novels and science fiction. I also enjoy going for runs a few times a week.

Examiner: How did you develop that interest?
Test-taker: My father introduced me to reading when I was very young. He had a big bookshelf at home， and I started borrowing books from it. Over time， it just became a habit.

Examiner: Do you prefer reading physical books or e-books?
Test-taker: I actually prefer physical books. There's something about the smell of paper and the feeling of turning pages that e-books just can't replace.
```

### P1-04: Technology

```
Examiner: How often do you use your smartphone?
Test-taker: Quite often， to be honest. I probably check it every hour or so， mostly for messaging and social media.

Examiner: Do you think people rely too much on technology?
Test-taker: I think there's some truth to that. Many people， including myself， might feel lost without their phones. But at the same time， technology has made our lives much more convenient.
```

## Part 2 — Cue Card 长篇独白（1-2分钟）

### P2-01: Describe a Person You Admire

```
Examiner: Here is your cue card. You have one minute to prepare.

"Describe a person you admire.
You should say:
- who this person is
- how you know them
- what qualities they have
- and explain why you admire them."

---

Test-taker (after 1 minute preparation):

I'm going to talk about my high school English teacher， Mrs. Zhang.

I first met her when I was in my second year of high school. She was assigned to teach our class， and at first I wasn't particularly interested in English. But her teaching style completely changed my attitude.

What I admire most about her is her patience and passion. She never got frustrated when students made mistakes. Instead， she would calmly explain the same point in three or four different ways until everyone understood. I remember once I was struggling with grammar， and she spent her lunch break helping me one-on-one. That really left a deep impression on me.

Another quality I respect is her ability to make learning enjoyable. She would bring English songs and short films to class， and we would discuss them together. Learning felt natural rather than like a chore.

The main reason I admire her is that she didn't just teach us English — she taught us how to learn. The study methods she shared are still useful for me today in university. Because of her influence， I became much more confident in using English， which has opened up many opportunities for me.

So， yes， Mrs. Zhang is definitely someone I look up to， not just as a teacher but as a role model.
```

### P2-02: Describe a City You Have Visited

```
Examiner: Here is your cue card. You have one minute to prepare.

"Describe a city you have visited.
You should say:
- when you went there
- what you did there
- what you liked or disliked
- and explain whether you would recommend it."

---

Test-taker:

I'd like to talk about Chengdu， which I visited two years ago during the summer holiday.

I went there with three of my university friends. We stayed for about five days. Since Chengdu is famous for pandas， the first thing we did was visit the Panda Research Base. It was incredible to see pandas up close， especially the baby pandas. They were even cuter than I had imagined.

Apart from the pandas， we also tried a lot of local food. Chengdu is known for its spicy cuisine， and we went to several hotpot restaurants. To be honest， the spice level was a bit challenging for me at first， but after a couple of meals I started to really enjoy it.

One thing I particularly liked about Chengdu was the laid-back lifestyle. People there seem to take life at a slower pace. We saw many locals sitting in tea houses， chatting or playing mahjong in the middle of the afternoon. It felt very different from the fast-paced lifestyle in my city.

If I had to mention something I didn't like， it would be the summer heat. It was extremely humid and hot in August， which made walking outdoors a bit uncomfortable.

Overall， I would definitely recommend Chengdu to anyone visiting China. It has a unique culture， amazing food， and a very welcoming atmosphere. I hope to go back someday.
```

## Part 3 — 深层次讨论（表达观点+理由+例子）

### P3-01: Education

```
Examiner: Do you think schools should focus more on practical skills rather than academic subjects?

Test-taker: I believe there should be a balance between the two. Academic subjects like math and literature teach critical thinking， which is fundamental. However， practical skills such as basic financial management or communication are equally important in real life. In my opinion， schools could integrate practical elements into existing subjects — for example， teaching statistics through real budgeting exercises.

Examiner: How has education changed in your country compared to the past?

Test-taker: Education in China has changed quite significantly over the past twenty years. When my parents were students， the focus was almost entirely on memorization and exam results. But now， there's more emphasis on creativity and independent thinking. Many schools have introduced project-based learning and extracurricular activities. That said， the pressure to perform well on college entrance exams is still very strong， so there's still room for improvement.
```

### P3-02: Technology and Society

```
Examiner: Do you think technology is making people more or less sociable?

Test-taker: I think it's a double-edged sword. On one hand， technology has made it easier to stay in touch with people who live far away. We can video call family members or chat with friends instantly， which was impossible a few decades ago.

But on the other hand， I've noticed that when people meet in person， many of them spend more time looking at their phones than talking to each other. In that sense， technology can reduce the quality of face-to-face interaction.

Overall， I don't think technology itself makes people less sociable — it depends on how we use it.
```

### P3-03: Environment

```
Examiner: What can individuals do to help protect the environment?

Test-taker: There are several practical things individuals can do. First， we can reduce our consumption of single-use plastics — things like water bottles and plastic bags. Second， using public transport or cycling instead of driving can lower our carbon footprint.

But honestly， while individual actions are important， I think the bigger responsibility lies with governments and corporations. Individual efforts alone can't solve problems like industrial pollution. We need stronger policies and greener business practices to make a substantial difference.
```

## 对话质量标准（用于评估 LLM 输出）

一个好的回答应该具备：

| 标准 | 说明 | 示例 |
|------|------|------|
| **自然展开** | 不只给一个短答案，加一点解释或细节 | "Where are you from?" → "I'm from Hangzhou. It's a city in eastern China known for..." |
| **逻辑连贯** | 使用连接词：because, although, for example, on the other hand | "I enjoy it because... Also, ..." |
| **词汇多样** | 避免重复用词 | 不是 "nice, nice, nice"，而是 "pleasant, enjoyable, satisfying" |
| **语法正确** | 主谓一致、时态正确、冠词使用 | "I have been" 而非 "I am been" |
| **语调自然** | 听起来像口语，不像是读书 | 用 "I think" "to be honest" "actually" 等口语化表达 |
| **长度适中** | Part 1: 2-3句; Part 2: 90-120秒; Part 3: 3-5句 | 不超时不超长 |
