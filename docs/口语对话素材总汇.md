### 1.5 评分标准（四个维度）

| 维度 | 说明 |
|------|------|
| Fluency and Coherence (FC) | 流利度、逻辑连贯性 |
| Lexical Resource (LR) | 词汇量、用词精准度 |
| Grammatical Range and Accuracy (GRA) | 语法结构多样性、准确性 |
| Pronunciation (P) | 发音清晰度、语调、重音 |

各维度评分细节对照：

| 维度 | 低分表现（4-5） | 中等表现（6-7） | 高分表现（8-9） | 系统反馈语示例 |
|------|----------------|----------------|----------------|--------------|
| FC | 频繁停顿，答非所问 | 偶尔犹豫，有逻辑连接 | 流畅自然，逻辑层次清晰 | "Your fluency was good but try using more linking words like 'furthermore' or 'on the other hand'." |
| LR | 基础词汇重复 | 使用了一些话题词汇 | 精准用词 + 习惯搭配 + 习语 | "Consider adding more precise terms like 'state-of-the-art' instead of 'very new'." |
| GRA | 简单句为主，语法错误多 | 复合句混用，偶有错误 | 复杂句灵活切换，几乎无错 | "Try incorporating more conditional structures like 'If I had...'." |
| P | 发音模糊，语调单一 | 清晰但有口音影响 | 清晰自然，语调有变化 | —（仅在语音模式下启用） |

### 1.6 Part 1 真题示例对话（3组完整）

#### 示例一：Work / Study

```
Examiner: Let's talk about what you do. Do you work or are you a student?
Candidate: I'm a student at Zhejiang University, majoring in Computer Science.
Examiner: Why did you choose to study Computer Science?
Candidate: I've always been fascinated by how technology can solve real-world problems, and I think programming is like learning a new way of thinking.
Examiner: What do you enjoy most about your course?
Candidate: I really enjoy the project-based modules — we get to build actual apps and websites instead of just reading textbooks.
Examiner: Is there anything you don't like about studying it?
Candidate: Sometimes the workload can be quite heavy, especially when multiple deadlines fall in the same week.
Examiner: Do you think you will work in this field after graduation?
Candidate: Yes, I'm planning to become a software engineer. I've already done an internship, which confirmed my passion for it.
Examiner: What kind of company would you like to work for — a big corporation or a startup?
Candidate: I'd prefer a startup, because I think I can learn more and have more responsibility from day one.
```

> **递进技巧观察**：考官从基本信息 → 选择原因 → 偏好细节 → 未来规划 → 对比选择，层层深入。

#### 示例二：Hometown

```
Examiner: Let's move on to talk about your hometown. Where are you from?
Candidate: I'm from Chengdu, which is the capital of Sichuan province.
Examiner: What's the most interesting part of your hometown?
Candidate: I think it's the food culture — Chengdu is known for its spicy Sichuan cuisine and the locals are very proud of it.
Examiner: Has your hometown changed in recent years?
Candidate: Definitely. The city has grown a lot — more metro lines, shopping malls, and high-rise buildings have appeared in the past five years.
Examiner: Is there anything you would change about your hometown?
Candidate: I wish the traffic were better. The city is expanding so fast that the infrastructure sometimes can't keep up.
Examiner: Do you think you'll always live there?
Candidate: I might move to a different city for work, but Chengdu will always feel like home and I'd love to return eventually.
Examiner: How does your hometown compare to other Chinese cities you've visited?
Candidate: Compared to Shanghai or Beijing, Chengdu has a more relaxed pace of life. People there really know how to enjoy themselves.
```

> **递进技巧观察**：基本情况 → 特色亮点 → 变化感知 → 批判思考 → 未来意向 → 对比参照。

#### 示例三：Hobbies

```
Examiner: Let's talk about hobbies. Do you have any hobbies?
Candidate: Yes, I enjoy photography and playing basketball in my free time.
Examiner: How did you first get into photography?
Candidate: It started about three years ago when my uncle gave me his old camera. I began taking photos of landscapes and gradually fell in love with it.
Examiner: What kind of photos do you like to take most?
Candidate: I prefer street photography — capturing everyday moments of people in urban settings. There's something very honest about it.
Examiner: Do you prefer doing hobbies alone or with others?
Candidate: I think it depends on the activity. I enjoy photography alone because it lets me focus, but I prefer playing basketball with friends because it's more fun.
Examiner: How much time do you spend on your hobbies each week?
Candidate: About four to five hours in total — maybe two hours for photography and two or three for basketball.
Examiner: Do you think hobbies are important for young people?
Candidate: Absolutely. Hobbies help us relieve stress and discover talents we didn't know we had. In my case, photography taught me to see the world from different perspectives.
```

> **递进技巧观察**：有无爱好 → 入门经历 → 具体偏好 → 对比选择 → 时间投入 → 观点升华。

### 1.7 Part 2 Cue Card 示例（2个）

#### 示例一：Describe a person you admire

**Cue Card 原文：**

```
Describe a person you admire.
You should say:
- who this person is
- how you know them
- what qualities they have
- and explain why you admire them
```

**示范回答（约 1.5 分钟，150 词以上）：**

> The person I admire most is my high school physics teacher, Mr. Chen. I got to know him when I was in my second year of high school, and he taught us physics for two years.
>
> What makes Mr. Chen special is not just his knowledge, but his teaching style. He had this amazing ability to make complex concepts like quantum mechanics or electromagnetic fields feel accessible and even fun. He would use everyday examples — like comparing electric circuits to water pipes — and suddenly everything made sense.
>
> But the quality I admire most is his patience. I remember one time I struggled with a problem about projectile motion for almost a week. Instead of just giving me the answer, he sat with me after class for three consecutive days, asking me guiding questions until I figured it out myself. He never made me feel stupid for not understanding.
>
> He also taught us values beyond physics. He often said that "the scientific method is not just for experiments — it's a way of thinking critically about life." That has really stayed with me.
>
> I admire Mr. Chen because he didn't just teach us a subject; he taught us how to think. He showed genuine care for each student's growth, and that kind of dedication is rare. Even today, when I face a difficult problem, I ask myself: "What would Mr. Chen do?" He inspired me to become not just a better student, but a better person.

**可选跟进问题（考官在独白后提问）：**

```
Examiner: Do you think teachers today are as dedicated as Mr. Chen?
```

#### 示例二：Describe a memorable event

**Cue Card 原文：**

```
Describe a memorable event in your life.
You should say:
- what the event was
- when and where it happened
- who was with you
- and explain why it was memorable
```

**示范回答（约 1.5 分钟，150 词以上）：**

> The most memorable event in my life was volunteering as an English teacher in a rural village in Yunnan province during the summer after my first year of university.
>
> It was a two-week program organized by my university's student union. A group of twenty of us traveled to a small village called Shuanghe, where the local primary school had only three teachers for over a hundred students. We taught English, music, and art — subjects the children rarely had access to.
>
> One particular moment stands out. On the last day, a shy little girl named Xiaomei gave me a hand-drawn card. She had drawn a picture of the two of us standing under a rainbow, with the words "Thank you, teacher — I want to learn English too" written in wobbly letters. I was so moved that I almost cried.
>
> This event is memorable because it completely changed my perspective on education. Before that, I took my own access to quality education for granted. Seeing how eager those children were to learn, despite having so few resources, made me realize how privileged I am. It also motivated me to become more active in community service.
>
> Since then, I've returned twice to similar programs, and I plan to keep doing it. That summer taught me that the smallest acts of kindness can have the biggest impact.

**可选跟进问题（考官在独白后提问）：**

```
Examiner: Do you think volunteer programs like this should be mandatory for university students?
```

### 1.8 Part 3 深度讨论示例（1组）

承接 Part 2 "Describe a person you admire"（关于老师的主题），进行深度讨论：

```
Examiner: Let's discuss education more broadly. What qualities do you think make a good teacher?

Candidate: I believe the most important quality is the ability to inspire students. A good teacher doesn't just deliver content — they make students curious. My physics teacher, Mr. Chen, was a perfect example. He could make even boring topics interesting by connecting them to real life. I think patience and empathy are equally essential, because students learn at different paces.

Examiner: Why do you think some students lose interest in learning as they grow older?

Candidate: I think it's partly because the education system becomes more exam-oriented. In primary school, learning feels like exploration — you do experiments, you ask questions. But by the time you reach high school, it's all about memorizing facts for tests. The pressure to perform kills the natural curiosity that children have. Also, if a student has had negative experiences — like a teacher who humiliated them — that can also turn them off learning permanently.

Examiner: How has technology changed the way students learn today compared to the past?

Candidate: Technology has made learning much more accessible. You can find a YouTube tutorial for virtually anything, which means students are no longer limited to what their local teachers can offer. Online platforms and AI tools also allow for personalized learning — if you don't understand something, you can repeat it or find alternative explanations. However, there's a downside: distractions. Many students find it hard to stay focused when their phone is full of social media and games. So technology is a double-edged sword.

Examiner: Do you think the role of teachers will eventually be replaced by AI?

Candidate: I don't think so, at least not completely. AI can certainly assist with grading, providing practice materials, and even explaining concepts. But teaching is not just about information transfer — it's about mentorship, emotional support, and inspiration. A machine cannot look at a student and tell that something is bothering them. It cannot share personal stories that resonate on a human level. I think AI will be a powerful tool for teachers, but the human element in education is irreplaceable.

Examiner: What changes would you recommend to improve the current education system?

Candidate: I'd suggest three things. First, reduce the emphasis on standardized testing and incorporate more project-based assessments that develop critical thinking. Second, invest more in teacher training — not just in subject knowledge, but in pedagogical skills and emotional intelligence. Third, bridge the gap between urban and rural education. The inequality in resources between big city schools and rural ones is staggering, and it's holding back millions of talented children. I think every child, regardless of where they're born, deserves access to quality education.
```

> **追问技巧分析**：
>
> | 追问类型 | 例句 | 作用 |
> |---------|------|------|
> | 直接扩展 | What qualities...? | 拓展同主题新维度 |
> | Why | Why do you think...? | 要求解释原因 |
> | How / What about | How has technology changed...? | 引入对比/新角度 |
> | 立场反问 | Do you think...AI...? | 要求判断+论证 |
> | 建议导向 | What changes...? | 从分析到实践 |

### 1.9 完整模考示例：雅思口语 Band 8 模拟测试（IELTS Advantage）

来源：YouTube "IELTS Speaking Mock Test - Band 8" by IELTS Advantage (17min)
角色：Examiner（考官，由 IELTS Advantage 老师扮演）, Candidate（考生 Rashmi，真实 Band 8）
话题：Part 1 - Study (MSC Marketing) / Free time / Travel; Part 2 - Describe the type of clothes you like to wear; Part 3 - Designer clothes / Work clothes / Dress code

```
--- Part 1 ---

Examiner: On this part I'd like to ask you some questions about yourself. Let's start with what you do. Do you work or do you study?

Candidate: Oh, I'm a student.

Examiner: And what subject?

Candidate: I'm studying MSC Marketing.

Examiner: And why did you choose that subject?

Candidate: Oh, because big data is everywhere and I had previous experience in SQL, so I guess I'd just come here and study analytics.

Examiner: And what do you hope to do with that degree after you finish?

Candidate: Oh, I hope to work as a data scientist.

Examiner: Why?

Candidate: Like I said, big data is everywhere and I really like SQL and data. I like everything about data, so this degree gives me the opportunity to study it in more detail than I knew previously. So I think yeah, it is a good opportunity to start a career in data analytics and data science.

Examiner: Okay, now let's talk about your free time. What do you like to do in your free time?

Candidate: Oh, I haven't really had too much free time, but usually I just like to read, travel, meet my friends, and I also volunteer on weekends.

Examiner: Do you prefer to spend your free time indoors or outdoors?

Candidate: Ah, it depends on the weather. Maybe if it's raining I don't like to go outside, but most of the time yeah, I would like to go outside rather than stay in my room.

Examiner: And what did you like to do in your free time when you were a child?

Candidate: Oh, I used to read a lot, just keep reading books all the time. So I never used to like go out and play much. I used to get scolded a lot by my parents, like "go out and play" — but I always wanted to stay indoors and read.

Examiner: And what are you going to do in your free time this weekend?

Candidate: Oh, this weekend, like I said, I volunteer for Macmillan Cancer Support, so I would be visiting patients and counseling them.

Examiner: So, let's talk about travel. Do you like to travel?

Candidate: Yes, I do.

Examiner: Why?

Candidate: Ah, because I like getting to know other countries, their culture, how people live, what they like, stuff like that. It's interesting to me.

Examiner: And where is a popular tourist destination in your country?

Candidate: Oh, in my country people mostly like to go to Europe because it's colder, and I come from a very warm country, so it's like a pleasant change for them. So most of us would like to go to America or anywhere in Europe where it's colder.

Examiner: Is traveling abroad very popular in your country?

Candidate: Oh yes, it is quite popular. People mostly go travel during the summer. I think movies have a huge role to play because they show destinations with snow-covered mountains, and that's very fascinating for us since we don't really get snow where I come from.

Examiner: And where would you like to travel to in the future?

Candidate: Oh, there are a lot of places in India that I haven't seen, so I might go there as well, and I like Bali and Greece.

--- Part 2 ---

Examiner: So I'm gonna give you a topic and I'd like you to talk about that topic for one to two minutes. You'll have one minute to prepare, and you can make some notes. Do you understand me?

Candidate: Yes.

Candidate: Okay. The type of clothing that I like to wear is mostly casual, but I wear different types of clothes on different occasions. In India we have a lot of festivals, so usually I wear casual clothes, but on festive occasions I wear traditional Indian clothes. I get them specifically made because you can't really buy traditional Indian clothes off the rack. I get them tailor-made so I can choose my own style. When you go to a temple, you wear pure simple clothes because that is the tradition, and that makes me feel really peaceful and calm. During Diwali and Holi, everyone wears white clothes because Holi has colors thrown on you — you go out with a white outfit and come back all colorful. When I go for an interview, I mostly dress formal, so that makes me feel very confident and prepared.

--- Part 3 ---

Examiner: All right, so we've been talking about clothing, and now I'd like to talk about designer clothes. Are designer clothes popular in your country these days?

Candidate: Oh yes, I think so. Designer clothes are usually very expensive, so only people of a certain socio-economic class can buy them. So they are popular in certain segments of society.

Examiner: And why do you think people buy designer clothes?

Candidate: Oh, I think because it has a social status attached to it. When you are wearing a specific label, people recognize it, and it shows what kind of class you belong to. It is a huge thing in India. It shows your affluence.

Examiner: So now let's talk about working clothes. Do you think you can judge someone based on the kind of clothes they wear at work?

Candidate: I personally don't, but I think there is a certain image attached to what you're wearing, and people build up perspectives based on that.

Examiner: Should people be forced to wear certain clothes at work?

Candidate: Oh well, they shouldn't be forced, but I think ever since we were young, we are told to wear certain clothes in certain settings because it's appropriate. In schools we wear uniforms, when we go to a temple we have a certain set of clothes. So I think we get used to dressing a certain way in a certain setting. As individuals, we should be aware enough of what is appropriate in our social setting.

Examiner: Have work clothes changed in the last couple of decades?

Candidate: I think it has become less formal in a lot of settings. Companies like Google have made it really popular for engineers to go to the office wearing casual clothes. They lay more importance on your work ethic and how you contribute, rather than what you wear. So it's a refreshing change.
```

### 1.10 完整模考示例：雅思口语全真模拟测试

来源：YouTube "IELTS Speaking Test Full Part 1,2,3 || Real Test" (37min)
角色：Examiner（考官 Phil Smith / Judith Harvey）, Candidate（三位不同考生）
话题：Part 1 - Work/Study / Weekends / Music; Part 2 - Describe a special gift you gave to someone; Part 3 - Gift-giving in families / International Aid / Business gifts / Economy

该视频包含三次完整的 IELTS 口语模拟测试，由三位不同考生完成。

---

#### 考生一：Sian Hassan（来自叙利亚，英语文学专业）

```
--- Part 1 ---

Examiner: Good morning, good morning. My name is Phil Smith. Can you tell me your full name, please?

Candidate: My name is Sian Hassan.

Examiner: Thank you. And can you tell me where you're from?

Candidate: I'm from Syria.

Examiner: Can I see your identification, please?

Candidate: Yes, sure.

Examiner: Thank you, that's fine. Now in this first part, I'd like to ask you some questions about yourself. Let's talk about what you do. Do you work or are you a student?

Candidate: I'm a student.

Examiner: And what subject are you studying?

Candidate: English literature.

Examiner: Why did you choose this subject?

Candidate: Well, I like literature very much, and English is an international language, so English literature is one of the best majors I've ever studied. So I like it very much.

Examiner: What do you hope to do when you finish your studies?

Candidate: Well, I'm planning to complete my higher studies. I'll be doing an MA, and eventually I'll be doing a PhD to become a professor in English literature.

Examiner: Now let's talk about weekends. What do you usually do at the weekend?

Candidate: Well, usually I go to visit my relatives or friends, but sometimes I stay at home if I have exams. But most of my time I spend with my friends, going out for a drink or to have fun.

Examiner: What do you think you'll do next weekend?

Candidate: Well, next weekend I have to go on a trip to York and I'm so excited about it!

Examiner: Why?

Candidate: Because I've heard that York is a very ancient city — the Vikings were there — and I like history.

Examiner: Do you enjoy your weekends now more than you did when you were a child?

Candidate: Well, not really. When I was a child, I just spent all my weekends playing. But now I have lots of concerns; sometimes I have to stay at home to study.

Examiner: How important is it for you to relax at the end of the week?

Candidate: Well, it is very important because you need to relax after the whole week of studying or working. You need peace and relaxation to prepare yourself for the next week.

Examiner: Let's talk about music. What sort of music do you usually enjoy listening to?

Candidate: Well, I like music very much because I'm a singer myself. I have great taste in music. I like classical, I like Arabic music. Music is very important — I have to listen to music all day.

Examiner: Has the kind of music you like changed over the years?

Candidate: Well, yes, it has changed. I used to listen to slow music, and nowadays it's very fast. I still listen to the music I liked when I was a child. I don't really like the new music.

Examiner: Do you prefer listening to live music or recorded music?

Candidate: Recorded. I don't like live music because I feel the singer is not good in live music. Recorded is better.

Examiner: Do you think listening to music helps you study?

Candidate: Yes, I always listen to music when I'm studying. Sometimes my friends make fun of me because they think I'm not concentrating, but actually it helps me concentrate.

--- Part 2 ---

Examiner: Thank you. Now I'm going to give you a topic and I'd like you to talk about it for one to two minutes. Before you talk, you'll have one minute to think about what you're going to say. You can make notes if you wish. Do you understand?

Candidate: Yes.

Examiner: Here's some paper and a pencil for making notes. And here's your topic: I'd like you to describe a special gift or present you gave to someone. All right, remember you have one to two minutes for this. Can you start speaking now, please?

Candidate: Okay. The special gift I gave was to my mother for Mother's Day. I went to different shops to search for what to give my mother. I decided to buy a very lovely necklace. It wasn't that expensive because I only had limited money. I asked the shop salesman to wrap it in colorful papers. I hid it until the party — we usually make a party for my mother on Mother's Day. When I gave it to her, she was really happy and almost in tears because she really liked it. She told me no matter how simple it is, the fact that I thought of giving her anything was really great. She wears it every time she goes out and always tells her friends, "Look what my daughter gave me for Mother's Day."

Examiner: Do you enjoy giving gifts?

Candidate: Yes, I really enjoy it. I like giving gifts to my friends and family because it shows that you are thinking of them and you really care for them.

--- Part 3 ---

Examiner: We've been talking about a special gift you gave to someone, and I'd like to discuss with you one or two more general questions related to this. Let's consider first of all giving gifts in families. On what occasions do family members give gifts to each other in Syria?

Candidate: Well, usually they give gifts on birthdays, on special occasions like Eid, at Christmas, and sometimes if anybody graduates from college or school. We usually give them presents and gifts.

Examiner: Do people tend to spend a lot of money on gifts?

Candidate: Sometimes, but it depends on the person you are giving the gift to and your own financial status.

Examiner: What sort of gifts do children give to adults in their families?

Candidate: This is really interesting because children always tend to think from their own view. If a small child is going to give his father or mother a gift, he tends to give a toy or something that the parent won't use, because the child thinks "I like this, so I'd like to give it to my father." So they always give from their own perspective.

Examiner: At what age do children start to choose gifts for their parents?

Candidate: It depends how the parents raise them. Before seven years old, they tend not to recognize what it means to give a gift. After seven, they start to feel that they need to give a present to their father or mother.

Examiner: How important do you think giving gifts is within families?

Candidate: It is important because you give them the feeling that you really care for them and think of them. It strengthens the relationships.
```

**Calling a Friend（smalltalk05 — 完整2变体）：**

**Variant 1（标准版）：**

```
A: Hello, may I speak to Alice please?
B: This is she. How's it going?
A: I've been trying to call you all day.
B: Sorry about that. I was cleaning up.
A: It's okay.
B: So what were you calling me about?
A: Oh, I just wanted to see if you wanted to hang out tomorrow.
B: Sure, what did you want to do?
A: Maybe we can go see a movie or something.
B: That sounds like fun. Let's do it.
A: I'll see you tomorrow then.
B: See you then. Goodbye.
```

**Variant 2（随意替换版）：**

```
A: Hi, how are you. Is Alice there?
B: Speaking. What's up?
A: Why haven't you answered the phone?
B: My bad, I had chores to do.
A: That's all right.
B: What was the reason for your call?
A: I want to do something tomorrow with you.
B: Sounds good. What did you have in mind?
A: I was thinking about seeing a movie.
B: Okay, let's go see a movie.
A: Until then.
B: Talk to you later.
```

> **关键变换**：
> - "May I speak to Alice" → "Is Alice there" → "Is Alice available"
> - "This is she" → "Speaking" → "You're talking to her"
> - "I was cleaning up" → "I had chores to do" → "I was busy"
> - "See you then. Goodbye" → "Talk to you later" → "I will see you tomorrow"

### 3.2 课程特点

**完整故事线：**

与大多数独立情景对话不同，VOA Let's Learn English **从第一课到第 52 课有一条完整的故事主线**：

- **主角 Anna** 从美国来到华盛顿特区，开始全新的生活
- 每课都是 Anna 日常生活中的一个章节，情节连续、人物关系逐步展开
- 主要角色包括 Anna、Jonathan（朋友/同事）、Amelia（朋友）、Pete（同事/上司）等
- 故事场景涵盖家、办公室、餐厅、商店、机场、公园、聚会等

**Beginner 友好设计：**

- 每课使用高频核心词汇，句子结构简单（SVO 为主）
- 语速适中（约 120 词/分钟），比真实语速慢
- 每课都有明确的语法重点（如 be 动词、一般现在时、过去时等）
- 对话句子短（平均 5–10 词），适合跟读模仿

## 四、系统场景配适建议

### 4.1 System Prompt 约束设计思路

为了让大模型输出稳定可控，system prompt 需要包含以下层次：

```
1. 角色定义       → "你是谁，你的唯一任务是什么"
2. 当前场景       → 动态填充的场景变量
3. 输出格式       → 严格规定回复格式（纯文本 / 无Markdown / 无emoji）
4. 对话规则       → 8条左右硬性约束（长度/语言/角色/纠错/边界/流程/反馈/禁止）
5. 边界处理       → 用户说中文怎么办、问无关问题怎么办
6. 示例对话       → Few-shot 示例
```

### 4.2 YAML 场景模板示例

**IELTS Part 1 — Hometown：**

```yaml
场景ID: ielts-p1-hometown
标题: IELTS Speaking Part 1 — Hometown
级别: B1
角色: You are an IELTS speaking test examiner. Conduct Part 1 of the test by asking questions about the test-taker's hometown. Follow up naturally based on their answers.
目标: Practice answering questions about hometown clearly and naturally.
关键词: hometown, where, like, change, best thing, would you move
评分标准: Fluency and coherence | Lexical resource | Grammatical range | Pronunciation
开场白: Let's talk about your hometown. Where is your hometown?
对话轮次: 6
示例对话:
  Examiner: Let's talk about your hometown. Where is your hometown?
  Test-taker: I'm from Suzhou, a city in Jiangsu province.
  Examiner: What do you like most about your hometown?
  Test-taker: I really enjoy the traditional gardens and canals there.
  Examiner: Has your hometown changed much since you were a child?
  Test-taker: Yes, it's become much more modern with new buildings and shopping malls.
  Examiner: Do you think you'll continue to live there in the future?
  Test-taker: I'm not sure. I might move to a bigger city for work opportunities.
```

**IELTS Part 2 — Cue Card (人物篇)：**

```yaml
场景ID: ielts-p2-admire-person
标题: IELTS Speaking Part 2 — Describe a person you admire
级别: B1-B2
角色: You are an IELTS speaking test examiner. Give the cue card to the test-taker, allow 1 minute preparation, then listen to their 2-minute response. After they finish, ask one follow-up question.
目标: Practice organizing a 1-2 minute monologue with clear structure.
关键词: admire, respect, qualities, influence, why
评分标准: Fluency and coherence | Lexical resource | Grammatical range | Pronunciation
开场白: Here's your cue card. You have one minute to prepare. "Describe a person you admire. You should say: who this person is, how you know them, what qualities they have, and explain why you admire them."
对话轮次: 2
```

**TOEFL Task 1 — Personal Preference：**

```yaml
场景ID: toefl-t1-preference
标题: TOEFL Speaking Task 1 — Personal Preference
级别: B2
角色: You are a TOEFL speaking test simulator. Present the prompt, then listen to the test-taker's response. Give ONE sentence of brief, constructive feedback focusing only on delivery or structure.
目标: Practice organizing a 45-second response with opinion + 2 supporting reasons.
关键词: preference, reason, example, opinion
评分标准: Delivery | Language use | Topic development
开场白: Here is your prompt. You have 15 seconds to prepare. "Some people prefer to study alone. Others prefer to study in a group. Which do you prefer and why?"
对话轮次: 1
```

### 4.4 IELTS 口语考试 System Prompt 参考

**Part 1 短对话模式：**

```
你是一位雅思口语考官。当前是 Part 1 阶段（Introduction & Interview）。
- 每次提出 1 个自然问题
- 根据考生的回答进行递进追问（Why? / How about? / What kind?）
- 确保总对话轮次不超过 {targetTurns} 轮
- 不要在对话中直接给出评分，记录在内部评估中
- 话题范围限定在：{current_topic}（如 Work/Study, Hometown, Hobbies）
```

**Part 3 追问策略：**

```
Part 3 追问策略（按优先级）：
1. WHY — "Why do you think that is?"
2. HOW — "How has this changed over time?"
3. COMPARE — "How does this compare to...?"
4. CONSEQUENCE — "What effects does this have?"
5. OPINION — "Some people say... What do you think?"
```
